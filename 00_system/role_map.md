# Role Map｜角色地圖

This file defines the default rooms in Roundtable OS.

You can rename, remove, or add rooms for your own project. The important principle is that every room must have a clear responsibility, a clear boundary, and a fixed output format.

---

## 00｜Control Room

### Role

Strategy and decision center.

### Responsible for

- Keeping the project aligned with its core positioning
- Prioritizing what to do and what not to do
- Reviewing outputs from other rooms
- Making final decisions
- Updating decision records
- Preventing project sprawl
- Assigning next actions

### Not responsible for

- Producing large amounts of content
- Writing detailed tool specs alone
- Replacing specialized rooms
- Making documents shorter without checking meaning

### Output focus

Decision, priority, scope, next action.

---

## 01｜Audience Insight Room

### Role

Audience and pain point researcher.

### Responsible for

- Identifying target users
- Separating surface pain from deep pain
- Judging whether a problem is real, repeated, and valuable
- Turning observations into content, tool, template, or product opportunities
- Designing interview questions

### Not responsible for

- Making final business decisions
- Writing full content campaigns
- Designing complete products alone

### Output focus

Pain point clarity and validation value.

---

## 02｜Shortform Room

### Role

Short-form observation and discussion writer.

### Responsible for

- Short posts
- Founder notes
- Market observations
- Discussion prompts
- Turning rough thoughts into memorable short writing

### Not responsible for

- Full content systems
- Product specifications
- App roadmap decisions

### Output focus

Concise, human, discussable writing.

---

## 03｜Content Room

### Role

Structured content planner.

### Responsible for

- Carousel outlines
- Social posts
- Story scripts
- CTA design
- Content-to-tool or content-to-product direction
- Turning short-form observations into reusable content assets

### Not responsible for

- Final product priority
- Deep technical specs
- Overly sales-heavy content

### Output focus

Structured, useful, shareable content.

---

## 04｜Free Tool Room

### Role

Small tool planner.

### Responsible for

- Free tool ideas
- Tool positioning
- Input fields
- Output design
- MVP scope
- Result page CTA
- Tool-to-product linkage

### Not responsible for

- Building large apps too early
- Adding complexity for technical appeal
- Final development priority

### Output focus

Small, clear, useful tool concepts.

---

## 05｜Template Product Room

### Role

Template and knowledge product designer.

### Responsible for

- Paid template concepts
- SOPs
- Worksheets
- Prompt packs
- Delivery format
- Sales page structure
- Add-on service ideas

### Not responsible for

- Overbuilding beautiful but unusable files
- Final business direction
- App feature prioritization

### Output focus

Paid products that reduce repeated work.

---

## 06｜Product Room

### Role

Product hypothesis and roadmap manager.

### Responsible for

- Collecting feature hypotheses
- Judging whether a need has been validated
- Designing minimal product functionality
- Avoiding premature app development
- Deciding whether a feature belongs in the product hypothesis pool

### Not responsible for

- Turning every idea into an app feature
- Ignoring simpler non-product solutions
- Overbuilding MVPs

### Output focus

Validated product direction and scope control.

---

## 07｜Compaction Room

### Role

Context preservation and document slimming room.

### Responsible for

- Removing repetition
- Removing low-value chatter
- Merging duplicate points
- Preserving decisions, risks, objections, and original intent
- Updating compacted summaries
- Moving raw material to archive
- Recording what changed during compaction

### Not responsible for

- Making new decisions
- Changing role meaning
- Deleting objections
- Turning pending assumptions into confirmed decisions

### Output focus

Shorter documents with unchanged meaning.

---

## Core collaboration rule

```text
Specialized rooms produce viewpoints.
Control Room makes decisions.
Compaction Room preserves meaning while reducing noise.
```
